2022-11-15T07:40:00+11:00 : Nov 15 (Reuters) - Commonwealth Bank of Australia  (CBA) , the country's largest bank, on Tuesday posted a 13.6% rise in its first-quarter cash earnings, helped by lending volume growth and a rising interest rate environment. 	Cash profit, which excludes one-offs and non-cash accounting items, rose to A$2.50 billion ($1.68 billion) in the three months ended Sept. 30, compared with A$2.20 billion a year ago and a Visible Alpha consensus of A$2.33 billion.
