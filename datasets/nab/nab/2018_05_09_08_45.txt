2018-05-09T08:45:00+10:00 : May 9 (Reuters) - Commonwealth Bank of Australia  (CBA)  posted a 2 percent fall in cash profit from a year ago as regulatory charges mount as a result of major failings in compliance and culture revealed by an independent inquiry.    Cash earnings, a measure that excludes one-offs and non-cash accounting items, fell to A$2.35 billion ($1.75 billion) for the three months ended March 31 compared with A$2.4 billion reported a year ago, the bank said in a limited trading update. 	Impaired assets for the quarter fell to A$6.6 billion, compared with A$6.7 billion last year.   ($1 = 1.3419 Australian dollars)
