2021-05-06T08:10:00+10:00 : May 6 (Reuters) - National Australia Bank Ltd  (NAB)  reported a 95% surge in first-half cash profit on Thursday as a rapid economic recovery encouraged the country's third-biggest lender to reverse provisions set aside for likely bad debt due to the pandemic. 	Cash profit came in at A$3.34 billion ($2.59 billion) for the half year compared with A$1.72 billion a year earlier, beating an estimate of A$3.05 billion according to a Reuters poll. ($1 = 1.2908 Australian dollars)
