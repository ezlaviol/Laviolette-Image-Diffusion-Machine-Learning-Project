2023-05-04T08:10:00+10:00 : May 4 (Reuters) - National Australia Bank  (NAB)  on Thursday reported a 17% jump in its first-half cash profit, as the country's second-largest bank continued to benefit from a high interest rate macro environment which in turn boosted its margins. 	NAB reported cash earnings of A$4.07 billion ($2.72 billion) for the six months to March 31, compared with A$3.48 billion a year earlier. Analysts on average had expected A$4.15 billion, according to Refinitiv data.	 ($1 = 1.4990 Australian dollars)
