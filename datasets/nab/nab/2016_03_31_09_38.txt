2016-03-31T09:38:00+11:00 : Outlook: Aus shares set to open higher
March 31, 2016 08:34 AM 

Following positive leads, the Australian sharemarket looks set to open higher.
Shares in Europe and on Wall Street closed stronger as global equities rally in the wake of US Fed Chair Janet Yellen’s cautious stance.
The US Dollar extended losses, meanwhile oil prices saw moderate gains. 

Markets 
Wall Street closed up yesterday:
The Dow Jones Industrial Average gained 0.5 per cent to close at 17,717,
the S&P 500 added 0.4 per cent to close at 2,064
and the NASDAQ gained 0.5 per cent to close at 4,869.

European markets closed up:
London’s FTSE gained 1.6 per cent,
Paris gained 1.8 per cent
and Frankfurt gained 1.6 per cent.

Asian markets closed mixed:
Tokyo’s Nikkei lost 1.3 per cent,
Hong Kong’s Hang Seng gained 2.2 per cent,
and China’s Shanghai Composite gained 2.8 per cent.

The Australian share market closed higher yesterday:
The S&P/ASX 200 Index closed 6 points up to finish at 5,010.

On the futures market the SPI is 42 points up. 

Currencies 
The Australian Dollar at 7:30AM was buying $US0.7674 cents, 53.36 Pence Sterling, 86.27 Yen and 67.68 Euro cents.
